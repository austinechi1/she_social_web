We See You

You didn't come this far to be
invisible online.

You are exceptional at what you do. Your clients rave about you. Your work speaks for itself. But if new clients can't find you online, that expertise stays hidden — and someone less qualified with a better social presence wins the business you deserved.

01

You're running your business AND trying to run social media

You're a doctor, a coach, a stylist, a CEO. Your time is worth hundreds of dollars an hour. Spending it figuring out what to post on Instagram is not the highest use of your genius. Yet here you are, staring at a blank caption at 10pm — exhausted — knowing you need to post something.

02

You know you need it but don't know where to start

Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, LinkedIn — every platform says it's the most important one. The information is overwhelming, the advice is conflicting, and by the time you've watched three tutorials, nothing has been posted and two weeks have gone by. Again.

03

Inconsistency is quietly costing you clients

You post when you have time — which means sometimes nothing for three weeks. Potential clients check your social media and wonder if you're still in business. Your competitors who post consistently are building the trust you deserve to have. Consistency isn't optional anymore — it's your reputation.

04

AI tools don't do it for you — they just create more work

You've tried ChatGPT for captions. You've tried Canva templates. But someone still has to write the brief, edit the output, design the graphic, write the caption, schedule it, and engage with the comments. AI is a tool. Strategy, execution, and showing up consistently? That still requires human hands and a real plan.

05

You don't know if what you're posting is actually working

You post something, get a few likes, and have no idea whether it moved the needle. Without strategy, social media feels like shouting into a void. No clarity on who you're reaching, what's converting, or what to do differently. That uncertainty makes it even easier to stop showing up.

06

Your brand deserves to look as polished as your work

You have a premium business. Your office is beautiful. Your client experience is exceptional. But your social media looks scattered — inconsistent colors, random posts, no cohesive story. Your online presence should reflect the level of excellence you bring to everything else. Right now, there's a gap.

Meet the Founder

The woman behind S.H.E. Social

Before S.H.E. Social, Sarah Mitchell spent 15 years leading social strategy for Fortune 500 brands like Nike, Sephora, and Google. She built teams, managed multi-million dollar campaigns, and learned exactly what it takes to make social media actually drive business.

But something kept nagging at her. The brilliant women she knew — the ones running their own practices, coaching businesses, creative studios — were struggling with the exact same strategies she'd perfected. They had the expertise. They had the clients who loved them. But they were invisible online because they simply didn't have the time or team to execute.

"I kept thinking: if I can do this for multi-billion dollar companies, why can't I do it for the women who actually deserve to be seen?"

So in 2020, she left the corporate world and started S.H.E. Social with one belief: exceptional women running exceptional businesses deserve exceptional social media — without having to become social media managers themselves.
